## 5. FAQs (snippet format)
- **`<h3>How much should a real estate agent spend on marketing?</h3>`** Most agents invest roughly 10% of their commission income into marketing, though new agents often spend more upfront to build visibility. Start with a lean budget focused on a website, one paid channel, and a CRM, then reinvest as leads convert into closings.
- **`<h3>What is the best marketing strategy for a new real estate agent?</h3>`** For new agents, the fastest path is combining a defined niche, a Google Business Profile, and consistent posting on two social platforms. Pair that with disciplined follow-up in a CRM. Referrals and local SEO compound over time and cost little beyond effort.
- **`<h3>Do real estate agents need a website?</h3>`** Yes. With more sellers now finding agents online than by referral, a website is the hub that captures leads and builds credibility. It hosts your listings, testimonials, and neighborhood content, and gives paid ads and social posts a destination that converts visitors into inquiries.
- **`<h3>How do real estate agents get leads online?</h3>`** Agents generate online leads through local SEO, a lead-capture website, social media, paid ads, and email nurture. Search and referrals produce the highest-intent leads, while paid ads accelerate volume. The key is consistent follow-up — most leads convert weeks or months after first contact.

## 6. Differentiation Notes
- **Framework over listicle:** Competitors (HousingWire, Matterport) publish idea lists; this pillar sequences tactics into a 7-step system plus a 90-day plan, giving the reader a path, not a menu.
- **Fresh, attributed data:** Uses current NAR stats (online vs. referral, millennial social preference, agent competition/failure rate) that most listicles omit or leave unsourced.
- **Budget benchmarks:** Adds concrete spend/tool guidance the top pages gloss over — a strong snippet and dwell-time win.
- **AEO-ready FAQs:** Snippet-optimized answers target the exact PAA questions to capture featured snippets and AI answers.

## 5. FAQs (snippet format)
- **`<h3>What is the most effective real estate marketing?</h3>`** The most effective real estate marketing combines local SEO, consistent social media, and disciplined lead follow-up. Search and referrals deliver the highest-intent leads, while video and professional listing media convert browsers into serious inquiries. Effectiveness comes less from any single idea than from doing a few consistently.
- **`<h3>How can I market real estate with no money?</h3>`** You can market real estate for free by optimizing your Google Business Profile, posting listing and neighborhood videos on social media, asking past clients for referrals and reviews, and answering buyer questions in blog content. These tactics cost only time but build compounding visibility and trust.
- **`<h3>What should real estate agents post on social media?</h3>`** Agents should post a mix of listing videos, neighborhood spotlights, market updates, client wins, behind-the-scenes content, and helpful buyer/seller tips. Aim for value over promotion — roughly 80% educational or local content and 20% direct listings — and stay consistent on two or three platforms.
- **`<h3>How do I stand out as a real estate agent?</h3>`** Stand out by owning a specific niche, building a consistent personal brand, and marketing with high-quality video and local content most agents skip. Fast follow-up, genuine community involvement, and a strong online presence signal professionalism and make you the obvious local choice.

## 6. Differentiation Notes
- **Categorized, not endless:** Competitors run flat "33 ideas" lists; grouping by channel plus a "choose the right ideas" filter reduces overwhelm and improves dwell time.
- **Budget-tier table:** A by-budget breakdown (free / mid / high) is a unique, highly shareable asset the top results lack.
- **AEO angle baked in:** A dedicated snippet/featured-answer idea and snippet-optimized FAQs target AI answers and PAA — on-brand for TheBaseo's AEO positioning.
- **Sourced stats:** NAR data points (millennial social preference, online vs. referral) add credibility most idea-lists omit.
