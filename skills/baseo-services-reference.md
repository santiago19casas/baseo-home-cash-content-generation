# baseo-services-reference

Service delivery cheat sheet for BASEO. The agency-world equivalent of a product UI reference: it documents **how BASEO actually delivers each service**, so creation skills (draft, outline) can describe processes accurately without inventing delivery details.

Maintained by Eddie / the BASEO delivery team. Read by `baseo-outline` and `baseo-draft`.

Update this file whenever delivery changes: new deliverables, changed timelines, new report sections, new tracking stack. Stale entries lead to hallucinated process descriptions in editorial content — the agency version of fake menu paths.

## 1. Diagnostic Audit

**What it is**: a free written audit of the prospect's site, competitors, and market, delivered before any monthly fee. The risk-reversal entry point and the universal CTA of all editorial content.

**Timeline**: delivered within ~2 business days of receiving the domain. Week 1, pre-engagement.

**What the prospect provides**: just their domain. No card, no contract, no call.

**Documented deliverables** (what writers can describe):

- The 3 biggest specific issues holding the site back (e.g., duplicate Carrot template content, missing schema on service pages, zero seller-situation pages, no tracking installed).
- Competitor gap: keywords the top local competitor ranks for that the prospect doesn't.
- Deal-math projection for the prospect's specific market.
- Pricing for their situation (custom per market) — included in the audit document, never published as a list.
- The document is the prospect's to keep, whether or not they sign.

**Honest-broker behavior** (safe and encouraged to state): if the market doesn't support the math, the audit says so. BASEO has turned down clients whose markets weren't a fit.

**Process shape**: review of site content/structure, tracking setup, GBP, competitor SERPs in the prospect's metro. `[PROCESS: tool stack and exact audit checklist not documented — use placeholder if an article needs that depth]`

**Limitations**: not a full technical crawl report dump; it's a written, prioritized document, not a 90-page PDF of tool exports.

## 2. Technical SEO + Foundation

**What it is**: the measurement and technical base layer, installed before content scales.

**Timeline**: months 1–2.

**Documented deliverables**:

- Call tracking installed (lead phone calls become countable).
- Form tracking installed.
- GA4 + Google Search Console configured (often for the first time in the client's history).
- Schema markup added to service pages.
- Existing template/duplicate content rewritten with original copy (typical first pass: the existing city pages).
- Google Business Profile rebuilt from scratch (categories, services, photos, posts cadence).

**End state after month 2** (safe summary): a site Google can read and a dashboard that shows leads, not impressions.

## 3. Programmatic City Pages

**What it is**: one original landing page per city/market the investor buys in. The primary lead-generating page type on investor sites (60%+ of organic traffic on successful investor sites lands on city pages, not the homepage).

**Timeline**: main buildout months 2–6, then ongoing expansion.

**Documented approach** (what makes them non-template, safe to describe):

- Each page is unique to its market: original local copy, local proof, market-specific data. Never swap-the-city-name templates (Google's Scaled Content Abuse policy specifically targets those).
- Built per market the client actually buys in; page count follows the client's footprint (a 1-city buyer and a 12-city buyer get different builds).
- Example scale from the flagship case study: 4 existing city pages rewritten in months 1–2; 18 new city pages built months 2–6.

## 4. Seller-Situation Content

**What it is**: pillar pages targeting the searches motivated sellers type under stress. These capture the highest-intent long-tail traffic on investor sites.

**Timeline**: months 2–6, then ongoing.

**Documented situation set** (the canonical five, safe to list): probate, foreclosure/pre-foreclosure, divorce, inherited property, problem tenants. Extended set when relevant: fire damage, code violations, tired landlord.

**Documented rationale** (safe to state): these pages convert above-average because the searcher's situation is urgent and specific; they also feed internal links to city pages.

## 5. AI Search Optimization (AEO)

**What it is**: making the client's pages the ones ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews cite when a seller asks how to sell fast for cash in their city.

**Timeline**: layered on from month 3, ongoing. Runs on accounts by default.

**Documented deliverables**:

- Content reformatted with Q&A structure and citation-friendly schema.
- **AI-citation tracking**: every city the client operates in, checked weekly, across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and AI Overviews. The client sees when they're cited (and when a competitor is).
- **AI referral attribution**: recovering AI-referred visits mislabeled as "Direct" in GA4, so the real AI numbers show in reporting.
- **AI Overview tracking**: weekly monitoring of every tracked keyword in the client's market that triggers a Google AI Overview, with alerts when a competitor gets cited as the source.

**Documented results context** (use Approved claims library in `baseo-context` for specifics): flagship client's pages began being cited by AI Overviews and Perplexity by month 7.

**Limitations**: no one controls whether an AI cites a page; we optimize the inputs and track the outputs. Never promise citation.

## 7. Lead-Focused Reporting

**What it is**: the monthly report and the accountability layer. Built around business numbers, not vanity metrics.

**Timeline**: monthly, every account.

**Documented report contents** (safe to list — this is public positioning):

- Organic cost-per-lead trend.
- Pages published that month.
- AI citations recorded.
- Next month's plan in plain English.

**Documented extra**: lead-intent classification — every form submission scored against motivated-seller signals so the client's team calls the highest-intent leads first.

## 8. PPC Management

**What it is**: Google Ads management for motivated-seller campaigns, offered as an optional complement to the organic program. The strategic frame is always organic-first: PPC keeps the phone ringing while SEO compounds, then paid spend shrinks as organic takes over.

**Timeline**: optional, can start alongside any phase of the engagement.

**Documented positioning** (safe to state):

- Run under the same lead-focused lens as organic: leads with phone numbers, CPL, cost per deal — never impressions or clicks as success metrics.
- Niche-specific: motivated-seller keywords and negatives, not generic real estate campaigns.
- The public expectation: most clients cut PPC spend 30–50% by months 6–9 as organic does the heavy lifting; some keep a small spend for emergency keywords or new markets. We never tell clients to kill PPC on day one.
- Same accountability mechanics as the rest of the engagement: written SOW, monthly reporting, month-to-month.

## Engagement timeline summary (safe editorial skeleton)

This month-by-month arc is documented via the flagship case study and public positioning; writers may use it as the canonical "what working with BASEO looks like" timeline:

| Phase | When | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| Audit | Week 1, free | Written audit: 3 biggest issues, competitor gap, deal math. Keep it either way. |
| Foundation | Months 1–2 | Tracking live (calls, forms, GA4, GSC), template content replaced, GBP rebuilt. |
| Content engine | Months 2–6 | City pages + seller-situation pillars published. First leads typically month 3–5. |
| AI layer | Months 3–9 | Q&A formatting, citation-friendly schema, editorial backlinks. AI citations typically visible months 6–9. |
| Compounding | Month 9+ | Lead volume that changes how the business feels. Flagship client: 28 leads, 3 deals, $54K profit in month 9. |

**Expectation language writers must mirror** (public FAQ): first leads typically month 3–5; real lead volume month 6–9; "anyone telling you leads in 30 days is talking about PPC or lying."
