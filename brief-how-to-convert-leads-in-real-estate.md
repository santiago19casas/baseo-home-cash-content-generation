## 5. FAQs (formato snippet)

**`<h3>What is a good lead conversion rate in real estate?</h3>`**
A good real estate lead conversion rate depends on the source. Purchased online leads convert at roughly 0.4%–1.2%, all sources average 2%–5%, well-filtered motivated seller leads convert at 10%–15%, and referrals reach 14%–30%. Investors should track lead-to-contract by source, not in aggregate.

**`<h3>How fast should you respond to a real estate lead?</h3>`**
Within five minutes. Agents who respond in under five minutes are up to 21x more likely to qualify a lead than those who wait 30 minutes, and 78% of customers buy from whoever replies first. For motivated sellers, minutes instead of hours can double conversion.

**`<h3>How many times should you follow up with a seller lead?</h3>`**
Most real estate deals close between the 6th and 10th follow-up, spread across 30 to 90 days — yet most investors quit after the second touch. Use a fixed cadence with new information at every touch: a comp, a market change, or a different deal structure.

**`<h3>Why are my real estate leads not converting?</h3>`**
The four usual causes: you respond too slowly, you stop following up after two touches, you talk price before uncovering motivation, or your leads are resold to several investors at once. Fix response time first — it is the cheapest and fastest lever.

**`<h3>Are paid real estate leads worth it?</h3>`**
Sometimes, but only if you measure cost per deal, not cost per lead. Purchased leads convert at 0.4%–1.2% and are often sold to multiple investors. Organic inbound leads from search convert far higher because the seller found you and contacted you first.

**`<h3>What is the best CRM for converting real estate leads?</h3>`**
The best CRM is the one you actually update. Investors commonly use REsimpli, Podio, Follow Up Boss, or GoHighLevel. What matters is not the brand: every lead needs a tagged source, a motivation tier, and a scheduled next action, plus instant SMS on form fill.

## 6. Notas de diferenciación

- **El SERP entero está escrito para agentes** (NAR, Placester, The Close, Luxury Presence, Wise Agent): buyer consultations, MLS, open houses. Ningún resultado del top 5 escribe para el investor que compra en cash. Escribir explícitamente para motivated sellers y cash offers captura el mismo keyword con cero competencia real de intención.
- **Táctica copiable, no consejos.** Los competidores dicen "follow up consistently". Esta pieza entrega el script de 6 preguntas, la cadencia de 90 días día por día, y la cadencia segmentada por tipo de seller (pre-foreclosure vs. probate vs. tired landlord). Eso es lo que se guarda, se comparte y se enlaza.
- **Tablas rankeables:** benchmarks por fuente de lead y net-to-seller. Dos activos de snippet/AI Overview que el top 5 no tiene.
- **Capa AEO (%currentyear%):** conectar la conversión con la fuente del lead y con ser la marca citada cuando un seller le pregunta a un asistente de AI quién compra casas en su ciudad. Es el puente natural al servicio de TheBaseo sin que la pieza se lea como un pitch.
