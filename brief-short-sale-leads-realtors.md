## 5. FAQs (formato snippet)

`<h3>What is a short sale lead in real estate?</h3>`
A short sale lead is a homeowner who owes more on their mortgage than their home is worth and is likely to sell for less than the loan balance with lender approval. These sellers are usually behind on payments or in pre-foreclosure, making them motivated prospects for agents.

`<h3>Where can realtors find short sale leads for free?</h3>`
Free short sale leads come from public records at the county recorder's office — search for Notices of Default, Lis Pendens, and trustee sale filings. The MLS, title companies, and referral networks with attorneys and lenders are also low- or no-cost sources, though they require more manual effort.

`<h3>Are short sale and pre-foreclosure leads the same?</h3>`
They overlap but aren't identical. Pre-foreclosure means the legal foreclosure process has started; a short sale is a resolution where the lender accepts less than the mortgage balance. Most short sales involve underwater, pre-foreclosure homeowners, but not every pre-foreclosure ends in a short sale.

`<h3>How much do short sale leads cost?</h3>`
It ranges widely. Public records are free, data-list subscriptions run roughly $40–$100 per month, direct mail costs about $0.50–$1.50 per piece, and live lead transfers can run $20–$60+ per lead. Measure cost per closed listing, not per lead, since one commission covers months of prospecting.

`<h3>Are short sale leads worth it for agents?</h3>`
Yes, for agents willing to work distressed niches. Short sale sellers are highly motivated, competition is lighter than standard listings, and foreclosure filings rose about 32% year-over-year in early %currentyear%. Success depends on fast follow-up, empathy, and understanding the lender approval process.
