## External authoritative sources

### Tier 1 (primary docs & primary data)

- [MIT / InsideSales Lead Response Management study (Dr. James Oldroyd, 2007)](https://www.revenue.io/inside-sales-glossary/what-is-lead-response-time) — supports: the 5-minute rule. 15,000+ leads, 100,000+ dials across six companies. Contacting a lead at 5 minutes vs 30 minutes: **100x the odds of contact, 21x the odds of qualifying**. ⚠️ **Attribution note**: this is routinely miscredited to Harvard Business Review. HBR's 2011 piece popularized the rule but its own numbers were different (42-hour average response time; 7x qualification advantage within an hour). The draft must credit **MIT/InsideSales (Oldroyd)** for the 21x, never HBR.
- [Real Estate Bees — Average Wholesale Assignment Fee (survey of 1,000+ wholesalers)](https://realestatebees.com/statistics/average-wholesale-assignment-fee/) — supports: **average assignment fee $13,000**; wide geographic spread (Arizona ~$5,000 low, North Carolina/Georgia ~$22,000 high). Original survey data, national sample.

### Tier 2 (original research & niche authorities)

- [Seer Interactive — How Traffic from ChatGPT Converts](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/case-study-6-learnings-about-how-traffic-from-chatgpt-converts) — supports: **ChatGPT traffic converts at 15.9% vs 1.76% for Google organic** (Perplexity 10.5%). GA4 data, Oct 2024–Apr 2025. Original methodology. This is the AEO layer's anchor number.
- [Real Geeks — How Much Do Real Estate Leads Convert?](https://www.realgeeks.com/blog/how-much-do-real-estate-leads-convert) — supports: **0.4%–1.2% purchased online leads; 2%–5% organic and referral**. The number the SERP's featured snippet currently uses.
- [Ylopo — Real Estate Lead Conversion Rate](https://www.ylopo.com/blog/real-estate-lead-conversion-rate) — supports: **referral leads 14%–30% vs portal leads 0.4%–1.2%**; the 10x–25x referral-to-portal gap.
- [Search Engine Land — ChatGPT ecommerce traffic converts 31% higher than non-branded organic search](https://searchengineland.com/chatgpt-vs-non-branded-organic-search-conversions-470321) — supports: independent corroboration that AI-referred traffic converts above organic. Backup for the AEO paragraph if Seer alone feels thin.

### Tier 3 (practitioners / niche vendors — use with explicit attribution)

- [Goliath Data — Motivated Seller Leads vs. Cold Prospects](https://goliathdata.com/motivated-seller-leads-conversion-rate) — supports: **well-qualified motivated seller leads convert at 10%–15%** (≈15–30 leads per deal); **cold lists 3%–5%** (≈25–35 leads per deal); pre-qualified leads go contract in **14–21 days** vs **45–60 days** for cold lists. The only investor-side benchmark set that exists publicly. Vendor blog, so attribute by name in the text.
- [iSpeedToLead — Motivated Seller Leads 2026](https://ispeedtolead.com/blog/motivated-seller-leads-2026/) — supports: motivated-seller definitions and the distress triggers behind the hot/warm/cold tiers. Qualitative use only.
- [LeadResponse — Speed-to-Lead Statistics](https://leadresponse.co/blog/speed-to-lead-statistics) — supports: the **78% of customers buy from whoever responds first** figure, which traces to **Lead Connect** research. ⚠️ The primary Lead Connect study is not publicly retrievable; every citation is secondary. **Recommendation**: keep it, attribute as "Lead Connect, as reported by LeadResponse," or cut it. It is not load-bearing. The MIT 21x number does the work.
- [Follow Up Boss — What's a Good Lead Conversion Rate](https://www.followupboss.com/blog/real-estate-lead-conversion-rate) — supports: agent-side benchmark corroboration.
