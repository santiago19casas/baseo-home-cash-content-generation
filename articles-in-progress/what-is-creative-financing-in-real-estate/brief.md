### H2: The Main Types of Creative Financing
- 50–70 word intro listing the methods; hand off to H3s. Note varying risk/effort.

#### H3: Seller Financing (Owner Financing)
- Seller acts as the bank; down payment commonly 10–20% (some 25%+), interest ~5–10%, term with a balloon. Buyer benefit: no bank underwriting, faster close, flexible terms. Buyer risk: balloon comes due, higher rate. Source the 10–20% figure.

#### H3: Subject-To Financing
- Buyer takes over existing mortgage payments; loan stays in seller's name; title transfers. Buyer upside: acquire with almost no cash, inherit a low rate. Real risk: due-on-sale clause (Garn-St Germain / 12 USC 1701j-3) lets the lender call the loan; concrete, not glossed.

#### H3: Lease Options (Rent-to-Own)
- Control now, buy later at a set price; option fee + rent credit. Fits investors who want control with little down; risk: you may never exercise / seller backs out.

#### H3: Loan Assumption
- Formal takeover with lender approval, releasing the seller (key difference vs subject-to). FHA/VA assumable loans; attractive when the existing rate is below market.

#### H3: Hard Money & Private Lending
- Short-term, asset-based, fast but expensive (higher rate + points). Fits flips/bridge, not long holds. Buyer/investor tool.

#### H3: Wraparound Mortgages
- Seller keeps original loan and writes a new larger loan that wraps around it; advanced; same due-on-sale exposure. Flag as advanced.
