### H1: Where to Find Foreclosure Listings in 2026
- Intro 60–90 words; keyword in first sentence; five categories framing; free vs paid promise; don't define "what is a foreclosure."

### H2: Quick Answer: The Best Places to Find Foreclosure Listings
- Snippet block. 2-line direct answer + 6–8 bullet list. 80–120 words. Must stand alone.
- **Snippet capture**: yes (list + paragraph).

### H2: Free Government Foreclosure Listings
- Bridge para (50–70 words): government sources are free and most reliable (real REO owners).
- H3s: HUD Home Store, Fannie Mae HomePath, Freddie Mac HomeSteps, VA & USDA.
- **Claims to use**: HUD 15-day owner-occupant priority; registered broker required to bid; Foreclosure.com/USHUD charge despite "HUD" in name.

### H2: Foreclosure Listings on Major Real Estate Portals
- Bridge (40–60 words): mainstream portals mix foreclosures into normal listings; filter to find them.
- H3s: Zillow, Realtor.com & Redfin, MLS.com.

### H2: Bank & Lender REO Listings
- BofA Real Estate Center, Wells Fargo (PAS/PEMCO), Chase. Free. Buying direct can cut intermediaries. 100–130 words. Keywords: REO, bank owned homes.

### H2: Foreclosure Auction Platforms
- Bridge (50–70 words): auctions = biggest discounts, biggest risk (sight-unseen, cash requirements).
- H3s: Auction.com, Hubzu & Xome.

### H2: Paid Foreclosure Listing Services
- Honest bridge (50–70 words): paid tools surface pre-foreclosure/auction data earlier but charge subscriptions; worth it for volume buyers only.
- H3s: Foreclosure.com, RealtyTrac.

### H2: How to Find Pre-Foreclosure Listings for Free (County Records)
- Star section / differentiator. 150–200 words. NOD, lis pendens, Notice of Sale defined. Dallas County example. Why it gives an edge.
- **Snippet capture**: secondary (definition list of the 3 documents).

### H2: Free vs. Paid Foreclosure Sources: Which Should You Use?
- Comparison table (Source / Cost / Best for / Update frequency) + recommendation by profile. 120–150 words + table.
- **Snippet capture**: table (secondary).

### H2: How to Verify a Foreclosure Listing Before You Act
- Listings go stale; verify via county records + official owner portal + listing agent. 90–120 words. E-E-A-T.

### H2: Ready to Sell a Property Fast Instead? (CTA)
- Soft cash-offer pivot for distressed homeowner readers. 60–80 words. Helpful, not pushy.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions
- 1-line intro; 5 FAQs each in its own H3, 40–55 word answers (provided in input).
