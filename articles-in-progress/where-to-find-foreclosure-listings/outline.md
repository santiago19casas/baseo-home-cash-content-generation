### H2: How to Find Pre-Foreclosure Listings for Free (County Records)
- **Word target**: 150–200 (depth differentiator — most words)
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. The channel competitors gloss over: go to the county recorder/clerk and search public foreclosure filings — free, often before anything hits a portal.
  2. Define the 3 documents: Notice of Default (NOD) = lender started foreclosure; lis pendens = judicial-foreclosure lawsuit filed (pending litigation); Notice of Sale/Notice of Trustee's Sale = auction scheduled with date.
  3. Example: many counties publish online, e.g., Dallas County posts foreclosure notices.
  4. Why it's an edge: you can reach the owner before the auction, while they still have options.
- **Internal link**: "what pre-foreclosure means for a buyer" → what-pre-foreclosure-means-for-buyers
