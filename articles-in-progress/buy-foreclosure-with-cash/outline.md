### H2: The Short Answer: No, Not Always
- **Word target**: 100–140

  1. Snippet paragraph (40–55 words, first paragraph): No — you don't have to pay all cash for most foreclosures. Pre-foreclosures, short sales, and bank-owned (REO) homes can be bought with a regular mortgage. Cash (or certified funds) is typically required only at the public auction.

#### H3: Pre-Foreclosure and Short Sales
- **Word target**: 90–120
- **Sub-bullets**: owner still holds title, selling before losing it; short sale = accepting less than owed, lender must approve; financeable with conventional or government loans; slow because of lender approval (short-sale approval can take months). #### H3: Bank-Owned (REO) Properties
- **Word target**: 90–120
- **Sub-bullets**: define REO (didn't sell at auction, reverts to the bank); the most "normal" financeable path; livable REO buys with conventional/FHA/VA like any home; sold as-is, so condition + appraisal can gate the loan; get preapproved early.
- **Data**: REO that's "basically livable" can be bought with a conventional mortgage; preapproval matters — [Policygenius](https://www.policygenius.com/homeowners-insurance/6-ways-to-pay-for-a-foreclosure-that-arent-cash/)

#### H3: Foreclosure Auctions
- **Word target**: 90–120

- **Data**: full price in certified funds within 24–72h, no inspection — [Auction.com](https://www.auction.com/blog/how-to-buy-foreclosures-at-auction/)

### H2: Why Auctions Usually Require Cash
- **Word target**: 110–150
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. The mechanical why (the gap competitors skip): a lender can't fund a mortgage without an appraisal, an inspection, and a clear title, and it can't do any of that in the 24–72 hours you have to settle.
     - Data: auctions settle in 24–72h with certified funds, sold as-is with no title report — [Auction.com](https://www.auction.com/blog/how-to-buy-foreclosures-at-auction/)
  2. Contrast the clocks: a normal mortgage takes ~30–45 days to underwrite; the auction gives you hours. The timelines don't overlap.
     - Data: regular mortgages run ~30–45 days — [Rocket Mortgage](https://www.rocketmortgage.com/learn/fha-203k-loan)
  3. Result: the sale settles in certified funds. It's underwriting math, not a rule someone invented to keep you out.
- **BASEO reference**: none.
- **Visual element**: pull-quote candidate — "A mortgage takes 30–45 days to underwrite. An auction gives you 24–72 hours. That gap is the whole reason auctions are cash."
- **Snippet capture**: no.

#### H3: Conventional Loans
- **Word target**: 70–100
- **Sub-bullets**: the standard route for a livable REO or short sale; preapproval is step 1; the home has to be in insurable/lendable condition (a hole in the roof or a missing furnace stalls it); typical down payment. Optional: Fannie Mae HomeStyle renovation (≈620 credit, as little as 5% down) wraps purchase + repairs.
- **Data**: banks won't lend on a house with a hole in the roof/missing furnace; HomeStyle ≈620 credit, 5% down — [Policygenius](https://www.policygenius.com/homeowners-insurance/6-ways-to-pay-for-a-foreclosure-that-arent-cash/)
- Keyword: conventional mortgage foreclosure.

#### H3: FHA Loans (3.5% Down)
- **Word target**: 90–120
- **Sub-bullets**: the low-cash path; 3.5% down at 580+, 10% down at 500–579; the home must meet FHA minimum property standards (safe, structurally sound, sanitary), which as-is foreclosures often flunk until repaired; **primary residence only, not investors or house-flippers** (state plainly).
- **Data**: 3.5%/580, 10%/500–579, FHA min property standards, owner-occupant — [Rocket Mortgage](https://www.rocketmortgage.com/learn/buy-foreclosed-home-with-fha-loan) · [Policygenius](https://www.policygenius.com/homeowners-insurance/6-ways-to-pay-for-a-foreclosure-that-arent-cash/)

#### H3: FHA 203(k) Renovation Loans
- **Word target**: 110–150
- **Sub-bullets**: the differentiator for beat-up foreclosures — purchase + repairs in one loan; Limited 203(k) up to $75,000 for non-structural work; Standard 203(k) for structural work, minimum $5,000, requires a HUD consultant; a licensed contractor must do the work and start within 30 days of closing; again **primary residence only** — not a flip vehicle.
- **Data**: Limited $75,000 / Standard $5,000 min + HUD consultant / licensed contractor / 30-day start / primary residence — [Rocket Mortgage](https://www.rocketmortgage.com/learn/fha-203k-loan) · [HUD](https://www.hud.gov/hud-partners/single-family-203k)

#### H3: Hard Money and HELOCs
- **Word target**: 90–120
- **Sub-bullets**: the investor's real tools; hard money = fast (funds in days), short-term, asset-based, high rates/fees, down payments up to ~30% — and the move that gets you into an auction: close like cash on hard money, then refinance into a cheaper conventional loan once it can pass a bank inspection; HELOC = pull equity from a property you already own for short-term cash. Warn on cost either way.
- **Data**: hard money asset-based/days/up to 30% down/refi play; HELOC up to 85% of equity, rates above a 30-yr mortgage — [Policygenius](https://www.policygenius.com/homeowners-insurance/6-ways-to-pay-for-a-foreclosure-that-arent-cash/)

### H2: How Much Cash Do You Actually Need at Auction?
- **Word target**: 130–170
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. There's no national number; it's set county by county. But the shape is consistent: a deposit the day of the sale in certified funds, then the balance fast.
  2. Example 1 (Philadelphia): a $5,000 deposit plus a $35 non-refundable fee just to bid; the deposit applies to a 10% down payment due the next business day, with the 90% balance due by the 15th day.
     - Data: [Philadelphia Sheriff](https://phillysheriff.com/real-estate/conditions-of-sale-mortgage/)
  3. Example 2 (Florida): 5% of your winning bid in certified funds immediately, nonrefundable, with the balance due the same or next business day.
     - Data: [Brevard Clerk](https://www.brevardclerk.us/foreclosures) · [Jefferson Clerk](https://www.jeffersonclerk.com/clerk-services/property-sales/foreclosures/)
  4. Example 3 (Lorain County, OH): win a $100,000 bid, hand over a $5,000 cashier's check on the spot, then bring the remaining $95,000 within 30 days — miss it and interest starts at 10%.
     - Data: [Policygenius](https://www.policygenius.com/homeowners-insurance/6-ways-to-pay-for-a-foreclosure-that-arent-cash/)
  5. The warning: miss the balance deadline and you forfeit the deposit. Verify *your* county's rules before you raise a hand.

### H2: Cash vs. Financing: Which Is Right for You?
- **Word target**: 120–160
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Cash: fast close, stronger negotiating position, the only way into most auctions. But it sinks your capital into one deal.
  2. Financing: preserves liquidity and lets you spread the same capital across several deals as down payments. But it's slower, and the house has to qualify.
  3. Operator deal-math: one all-cash purchase vs. the same cash as 20–25% down on three or four financed deals. Cash wins on speed and auctions; leverage wins on deal count.
  4. Self-classify: auction hunter and flipper who needs speed → cash (or hard money that acts like it). Buy-and-hold building a portfolio → finance the financeable stages.
  5. Either way, the bottleneck is usually deal *flow*, not capital — you still have to be the buyer sellers find first.

### H2: Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Word target**: 110–150
- **Sub-bullets** (bulleted list — satisfies list minimum):
  1. Assuming every foreclosure needs cash (you just talked yourself out of the financeable 90%).
  2. Bidding at auction without certified funds lined up or any inspection — you're buying blind and can't pay.
  3. Underestimating repairs — the "deal" evaporates when the rehab number is real.
  4. Skipping preapproval before you offer on an REO — cash-flush investors take the property while you're still filling out forms.
  5. Ignoring liens and back taxes that survive the sale — the one that quietly eats auction buyers alive; you can win the bid and inherit someone else's debt.
- **Data**: auction sold as-is with no title report / unknown liens — [Auction.com](https://www.auction.com/blog/how-to-buy-foreclosures-at-auction/); preapproval before bidding vs cash-flush investors — [Policygenius](https://www.policygenius.com/homeowners-insurance/6-ways-to-pay-for-a-foreclosure-that-arent-cash/)
