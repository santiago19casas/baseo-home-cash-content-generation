### H1: What Does Pre-Foreclosure Mean for a Buyer?
- **Original guideline**: Open with a 2-3 sentence direct answer for the snippet: pre-foreclosure is the window after a homeowner defaults but before the bank repossesses/auctions the home — for a buyer, it's a chance to buy below market by negotiating directly with a motivated owner. State up front that the home is still legally the owner's, not the bank's. Set expectation for what the article covers. ~80-120 words.
- **Persona-specific angle**: B4 wants the bottom line first. Lead with the direct definition, then one line on why an investor cares (below-market, direct negotiation), then the "still the owner's, not the bank's" fact. Intro proper stays under 80 words (Template C, relatable buy-side scene); the definitional paragraph can live under the first H2 for the snippet.
- **Service reference**: none (definition)
- **Claims to use**: none first-party. Anchor definition to a public source (Rocket Mortgage / Experian) sourced in research.
- **Snippet capture**: no (H1 sets up; the 40–60 word snippet lands under H2 #1)
- **Watch out for**: don't slip into seller-address ("if you're behind on payments"); the reader is the buyer. No "in today's market" opener.

### H2: Pre-Foreclosure, Explained in One Minute
- **Original guideline**: Define pre-foreclosure plainly: begins when the lender files a Notice of Default (NOD) after ~3-6 missed payments; ends when the home is sold, reinstated, or goes to auction. Emphasize the buyer-relevant fact: the homeowner still owns and controls the property and can sell it. Include keywords: Notice of Default, default, mortgage arrears. Cite a public source. 120-160 words.
- **Persona-specific angle**: Give the operator the mechanics fast. The buyer-relevant hook: the owner still holds the keys, so you deal with a person, not a bank.
- **Service reference**: none
- **Claims to use**: external — NOD timing (~3–6 missed payments), definition. Source: Rocket Mortgage / Experian / CFPB.
- **Snippet capture**: yes (paragraph) — 40–60 word definition as the first paragraph under this H2.
- **Watch out for**: agency-speak N/A here; keep the definition tight, don't over-explain.
