### H2: Pre-Foreclosure vs. Foreclosure vs. Short Sale

- **Word target**: 150–200 + table
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Pre-foreclosure = buy from the owner before auction; owner holds title.
  2. Foreclosure / REO = bank owns it; buy at auction or from the bank, often as-is, less due-diligence time.
  3. Short sale = selling for less than owed; needs lender approval; can overlap with pre-foreclosure.
  4. Short prose framing above the table; table does the comparison work.
- **External links**: definitions → [Rocket Mortgage](https://www.rocketmortgage.com/learn/preforeclosure); short sale → [Nolo](https://www.nolo.com/legal-encyclopedia/deed-lieu-vs-short-sale.html)

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

#### H3: Can you buy a house in pre-foreclosure?
- 40–60 words: Yes. You buy directly from the owner, who still holds title, before the auction — negotiating a price that satisfies the mortgage, or via a lender-approved short sale if the loan is underwater.

#### H3: Is pre-foreclosure a good time to buy?
- 40–60 words: It can be — often some discount and less competition than the open market — but deals run longer and carry more risk (liens, lender delays, seller backing out). Best for patient buyers who can do their diligence.

#### H3: How much cheaper are pre-foreclosure homes?
- 40–60 words: It varies. There's no fixed discount; price depends on the owner's equity, the home's condition, local demand, and how motivated the seller is. Pre-foreclosures usually sell for less of a discount than auction or bank-owned homes because the owner still controls the sale. Source: [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/learn/buying-a-preforeclosure-property/). **NO fixed %.**

#### H3: What's the difference between pre-foreclosure and foreclosure?
- 40–60 words: In pre-foreclosure the homeowner still owns the property and you negotiate with them before the auction. In foreclosure the lender has taken the home (or sold it at auction), so you buy from the bank or at the courthouse, usually with less time to inspect. Point to Table 1.

#### H3: Can a seller back out of a pre-foreclosure sale?
- 40–60 words: Before a binding contract, yes — an owner can reinstate the loan or find other financing and keep the home. Once a purchase agreement is signed, normal contract law applies. Confirm your protections with a real estate attorney.
