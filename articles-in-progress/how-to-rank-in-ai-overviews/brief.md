- 90–120 words. Scannable: descriptive question-form headings, lists, comparison tables, short paragraphs. How-to guides, tutorials, and comparisons with clear differentiators get cited most. - 100–130 words. AI reads topics via entities and relationships, not exact-match. Explain pillar + clusters + interlinking. Related entities (people, tools, concepts) add depth. Example: an investor AEO cluster linking city pages ↔ seller-situation pages ↔ "what triggers an AIO." **Service**: Programmatic City Pages + Seller-Situation Content (the cluster). **Watch**: no proprietary page counts.

- 100–130 words. E-E-A-T as central signal. Google favors first-hand practitioner explanations. Recommend: real author with bio + credentials, own case data, screenshots, grounded opinions. Difference between summarizing what exists vs adding something new (models prefer novel info). **Service**: soft — this is why an investor's real deal data/GBP reviews matter. **Watch**: no fabricated authority claims.

#### H3: 6. Add Schema Markup the Right Way
- 100–130 words. Schema doesn't guarantee inclusion but improves clarity/eligibility; Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Product, Organization appear in citation chips at higher rates. #### H3: 7. Add Original Data, Quotes, and Fresh Sources
