## Enriched heading structure

### H2: Why the Right Questions Matter More Than the Right Script
- **Original guideline**: 120-160 palabras. Explicar que un script rígido falla; lo que importa es entender qué revela cada pregunta. Marco mental: cada pregunta califica uno de cuatro ejes—motivación, condición, equity/finanzas, timeline. Enfatizar escucha activa: el seller habla 70%, el investor 30%. Evitar sonar transaccional. Keyword secundaria: "qualifying motivated sellers".
- **Persona-specific angle**: B4 implements, so give him the four-axis mental model as the reusable framework, not a slogan. Frame the 70/30 talk ratio as a discipline he can measure himself against.
- **Service reference**: none
- **Claims to use**: external stat candidate — ~80% of motivated-seller transactions happen after the 5th contact (source to verify); supports "qualifying is a process, not one script."
- **Snippet capture**: no
- **Watch out for**: "the power of," transactional agency tone, robotic list framing.

### H2: How to Open the Call: Rapport and Control
- **Original guideline**: 80-100 palabras. Primeros 60 segundos definen el tono: primero rapport (cumplido genuino sobre la casa, tono relajado), luego tomar control con preguntas suaves. No saltar a números. Puente hacia las dos sub-preguntas de apertura.
- **Persona-specific angle**: Practical: what to literally say in the first 60 seconds. Control without interrogation.
- **Service reference**: none
- **Snippet capture**: no
- **Watch out for**: sounding scripted; em-dashes.

#### H3: Are You the Decision-Maker?
- Model question verbatim; how to handle co-owners (spouse, heirs, partners). Payoff in the reader's units: don't burn 20 minutes on someone who can't say yes.

#### H3: How Did You Hear About Us?
- Ice-breaker that hands the seller the mic and reveals which marketing channel produced the lead. Light bridge foreshadow (channel attribution) — no BASEO mention yet.

### H2: Questions That Uncover Motivation
- **Original guideline**: 60-80 palabras de intro al bloque. El bloque más importante: sin motivación real no hay deal bajo retail. "Motivación" = razón con urgencia. Causas comunes: divorcio, pre-foreclosure, herencia, mudanza por trabajo, casa irreparable, cansancio de landlord.
- **Persona-specific angle**: This is the block B4 most often rushes; slow him down. Motivation = reason + urgency, not curiosity.
- **Service reference**: none (note: these same situations — probate, foreclosure, divorce, inherited, tired landlord — are what BASEO's seller-situation pages target; do NOT state that here, save the channel connection for the bridge H2).
- **Claims to use**: none (qualitative block)
- **Snippet capture**: no
- **Watch out for**: drifting into seller-addressed empathy; keep it investor-facing.

#### H3: Why Are You Selling?
- **Original guideline**: 90-120 palabras. LA pregunta central. Abierta: "What's got you thinking about selling right now?" Callar y dejar hablar. 2-3 ejemplos de respuestas y qué señalan. Keyword: "motivated seller questions".
- Keyword "motivated seller questions" lands here naturally.

#### H3: What Happens If the House Doesn't Sell?
- **Original guideline**: 80-110 palabras. La pregunta que separa a los que saben calificar. Pánico = alta motivación; indiferencia = baja. Enmarcar como de las más subestimadas del negocio.

#### H3: What's Your Timeline to Sell?
- **Original guideline**: 70-90 palabras. Timeline = urgencia. "Ideally, when would you want this handled?" Lectura: "ayer" = motivado; "algún momento este año" = tibio. Conectar timeline con capacidad de negociar precio.

### H2: Questions About the Property
- **Original guideline**: 40-60 palabras de intro. Estas preguntas estiman la reparación (ARV menos repairs) y complejidad del cierre antes de pisar la casa.
- **Persona-specific angle**: ARV-minus-repairs framing; B4 speaks this language.
- **Service reference**: none
- **Snippet capture**: no

#### H3: What Condition Is the Property In?
- Open question; push for specifics (roof, HVAC, plumbing, kitchen/baths, water damage). Sellers underestimate damage; verify in person.

#### H3: How Long Have You Owned It?
- Long ownership usually = more equity, sometimes more emotional attachment. Estimates margin for a below-retail cash offer.

#### H3: Is Anyone Living There Now?
- Vacant = simpler close; occupied (owner or tenant) = plan the move or handle the lease. Ask about the lease if tenant-occupied. Impacts timeline and exit.

### H2: Questions About the Money
- **Original guideline**: 40-60 palabras de intro. Aquí se calcula si el deal cierra con números. Hacerlas después del rapport, no al inicio.
- **Persona-specific angle**: The money block only works after motivation and condition; sequence discipline.
- **Service reference**: none
- **Snippet capture**: no

#### H3: Is There a Mortgage or Any Liens?
- Base for equity math. Define liens (back taxes, mechanic's, HOA) and why they matter for title. Model question, direct but soft.

#### H3: Are the Payments Current?
- Behind on payments = strong distress/urgency signal (possible pre-foreclosure). Handle with empathy, not "found a deal" energy.

#### H3: What Price Do You Have in Mind?
- **Original guideline**: 90-120 palabras. Deja que el seller ancle primero. "Have you had it appraised, or do you have a number in mind?" Concepto "squeal point" (precio más bajo que aceptarían) y por qué define el margen para el end buyer. No dar el primer número. Keyword: "motivated seller script".
- Keyword "motivated seller script" lands here.

### H2: Closing Questions That Set Up the Offer
- **Original guideline**: 40-60 palabras de intro. Confirman urgencia y preparan la transición a la oferta.
- **Service reference**: none
- **Snippet capture**: no

#### H3: If We Agreed on a Fair Price, How Fast Could You Close?
- Hypothetical that measures real commitment without committing the investor to a number. Fast, concrete answer = ready seller.

#### H3: What Form of Payment Do You Prefer?
- Open question that surfaces the seller's bottom line and needs (fast cash vs. more money over time). Can open creative-financing door.
