#### H3: Are You the Decision-Maker?
- **Word target**: 60–90
- Model question verbatim: "Is the house jointly owned, or are you the only one who'd make the decision to sell?" Handle co-owners (spouse, heirs, partners). Payoff: don't invest 20 minutes with someone who can't say yes.

#### H3: How Did You Hear About Us?
- **Word target**: 50–70
- Ice-breaker, hands the seller the mic, sets the precedent that they answer. Bonus: reveals which marketing channel produced the lead. Rephrase brief's "before we dive in" → "before we get into it" (banned phrase).

### H2: Questions That Uncover Motivation

- **Word target**: 60–80 (block intro)
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Most important block: no real motivation, no below-retail deal.
  2. Motivation = a reason with urgency behind it, not "just seeing what I can get."
  3. Common drivers: divorce, pre-foreclosure, inherited property, job relocation, a house they can't afford to repair, tired of being a landlord.
- **BASEO reference**: none (do NOT pitch seller-situation pages here — save for bridge)
- **Visual element**: none
- **Snippet capture**: no
- **Secondary keyword**: "motivated seller questions" lands in the H3 below

#### H3: Why Are You Selling?
- **Word target**: 90–120
- Open question: "What's got you thinking about selling right now?" Shut up and let them talk; the reason behind the reason shows up in sentence 2–3. Give 2–3 example answers + what each signals (e.g., "my brother and I inherited it and we live out of state" = high motivation + carrying-cost urgency).

#### H3: What Happens If the House Doesn't Sell?
- **Word target**: 80–110
- The question that separates real qualifiers. Panic ("I can't keep paying") = high; indifference ("I'll just keep it") = low. Frame as one of the most underrated questions in the business.

#### H3: What's Your Timeline to Sell?
- **Word target**: 70–90
- Timeline = urgency. "Ideally, when would you want this handled?" "Yesterday"/"before month-end" = motivated; "sometime this year" = warm at best. Connect timeline to negotiating room on price.

### H2: Questions About the Property

- **Word target**: 40–60 (block intro)
- **Sub-bullets**: these estimate repairs (ARV minus repairs) and closing complexity before you ever walk the property.
- **BASEO reference**: none
- **Visual element**: none
- **Snippet capture**: no

#### H3: What Condition Is the Property In?
- **Word target**: 70–90
- Open question, let them describe; then push for specifics: roof, HVAC, plumbing, kitchen/baths, water damage. Sellers underestimate damage — verify in person.

#### H3: How Long Have You Owned It?
- **Word target**: 60–80
- Long ownership usually = more equity (or the reverse: heavy emotional attachment). Helps gauge margin for a below-retail cash offer.

#### H3: Is Anyone Living There Now?
- **Word target**: 60–80
- Vacant = simpler close; owner- or tenant-occupied = plan the move or handle the lease. Ask about the lease term if there's a tenant. Impacts timeline and exit strategy.

### H2: Questions About the Money

- **Word target**: 40–60 (block intro)
- **Sub-bullets**: this is where the deal math closes or doesn't. Ask these after rapport, never at the top of the call.
- **Visual element**: table — the four-axis question map / or the equity-math mini table (see visual plan, Table 1)
- **BASEO reference**: none
- **Snippet capture**: no

#### H3: Is There a Mortgage or Any Liens?
- **Word target**: 70–90
- Base for equity math. Define liens in plain language (back taxes, mechanic's liens, HOA) and why they matter for clean title. Model question: "Do you still owe anything on it, a mortgage, or any liens or back taxes?"

#### H3: Are the Payments Current?
- **Word target**: 60–80
- Behind on payments = strong distress/urgency signal (possible [pre-foreclosure](https://www.thebaseo.com/home-cash-buyers-seo/resources/what-pre-foreclosure-means-for-buyers/)). Handle with empathy, not "I found a deal" energy.
- **Internal link**: "pre-foreclosure" → what-pre-foreclosure-means-for-buyers

#### H3: What Price Do You Have in Mind?
- **Word target**: 90–120
- Let the seller anchor first: "Have you had it appraised, or do you have a number in mind?" Introduce "squeal point" (the lowest price they'd realistically accept) and why knowing it defines your margin for the end buyer. Don't give the first number.
- **Secondary keyword**: "motivated seller script" lands here

### H2: Closing Questions That Set Up the Offer

- **Word target**: 40–60 (block intro)

#### H3: If We Agreed on a Fair Price, How Fast Could You Close?
- **Word target**: 70–90
- Hypothetical that measures real commitment without committing you to a number. Fast, concrete answer = ready seller. Ties timeline + motivation + willingness to act.

#### H3: What Form of Payment Do You Prefer?
- **Word target**: 50–70
- Open question that surfaces the seller's bottom line and needs (fast cash vs. more money over time). Can open the creative-financing door.

### H2: Mistakes That Kill the Call

- **Word target**: 120–160
- **Sub-bullets** (bulleted list, 5–6 errors, each with a one-line fix):
  1. Talking more than the seller → aim for 30/70.
  2. Jumping to price before rapport → earn the number.
  3. Sounding like a robot reading a script → know the reveal, not the words.
  4. Showing "I won" excitement → stay flat and calm.
  5. Not confirming the decision-maker → ask in the first two minutes.
  6. Firing closed yes/no questions → open-ended only.
