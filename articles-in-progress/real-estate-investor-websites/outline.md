### H2: Templated vs. Custom: The Hidden SEO Cost Nobody Mentions

- **Word target**: 190–230
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. The gap no competitor covers: when hundreds of investors run the same template, sites share near-identical structure and copy. A seller comparing two "we buy houses" sites sees the same layout twice — that erodes trust and differentiation.
  2. Cite the scale: 30%+ of top-ranking "we buy houses" sites run near-identical Carrot template content ([Carrot](.../how-ai-is-reshaping-seo-for-real-estate-investors-2/)). Pull-quote candidate.
  3. Why it can cap you in competitive markets: Google's March 2024 core update added the Scaled Content Abuse policy, targeting large amounts of unoriginal content "no matter how it's created" ([Google Search Central](https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2024/03/core-update-spam-policies)). Duplicate/near-duplicate pages don't differentiate. Entities: duplicate content, E-E-A-T, page experience.
  4. The AEO layer: AI Overviews now show on nearly half of searches ([Carrot](...)). Answer engines cite distinctive, well-structured pages; near-identical templates give them nothing to prefer. Entities: AEO, schema markup.
  5. The custom contrast: a site engineered for keyword architecture, speed, schema, and unique per-market content ranks and gets cited. **This is what BASEO builds for cash home buyers — a unique page per market, not swap-the-city-name templates — because duplicated pages are exactly what Google's policy targets.** (WHAT/WHY + that-we-do-it, no how.)
  6. Be fair: templates are the right call for speed and budget; custom wins for serious local SEO/AEO.
  7. **Mid-post CTA** right after: "Not sure whether your template is costing you rankings in your market? That's what a free audit answers."
