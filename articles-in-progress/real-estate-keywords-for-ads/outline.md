### H2: Match types: telling Google exactly what to buy

- **Word target**: 150–200
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Broad match — widest reach, most waste; example: broad `sell my house fast` can trigger on "how to sell my house" or "house cleaning fast." Translate at first use.
  2. Phrase match — the words in order, close variants allowed; example: "sell my house fast" triggers "sell my house fast in Dallas."
  3. Exact match — the term (and very close variants) only; example: [sell my house fast].
  4. Practical rec: start with phrase + exact to control spend; use broad only with Smart Bidding + a robust negative list.
  5. Tie to Quality Score: tighter match = more relevant = better Quality Score = lower CPC.
- **External links**: [Google Ads Help — keyword matching options](https://support.google.com/google-ads/answer/7478529?hl=en)
- **BASEO reference**: none

### H2: How to group these keywords into ad groups

- **Word target**: 130–170
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Rule: 10–20 tightly related keywords per ad group (Google's own new-ad-group suggestion), max ~30 when the theme is very tight; tighter (5–15) is fine.
