## Enriched heading structure

### H2: What makes a keyword good for ads (and different from SEO)
- **Original guideline**: 120–160 words. Explain that in PPC you pay per click, so a good ad keyword is transactional and specific, not high-volume informational. Direct contrast: "real estate market trends" (SEO/blog) vs "sell my house fast [city]" (ads). Introduce 3 criteria: conversion intent, specificity (long-tail), cost control. Name entities: buyer intent, transactional keywords, long-tail. Thesis: intent rules.
- **Persona-specific angle**: B2 gets the cost frame first (every click is billed, so the wrong keyword is a paid mistake). Keep it operator-blunt.
- **Service reference**: none (contextual PPC framing only)
- **Claims to use**: external stat candidate — "sell my house fast" CPC $25–$120/click; CPC rose ~36% 2023→2024 (source in dossier).
- **Snippet capture**: no
- **Watch out for**: "leverage," "the power of," agency-speak. Translate "conversions" → "leads."

### H2: Start with intent: the 3 buckets that decide your ROI
- **Original guideline**: 130–170 words. Present the framework: buyer, seller, investor (location as a cross-cutting layer). Grouping by intent → more relevant ads and landing pages → better Quality Score and lower CPC. Key note: one business may need only 1–2 buckets (an investor only wants seller-intent). Preview that long-tail/hyperlocal convert better. Prepare the reader to pick their buckets.
- **Persona-specific angle**: B4 picks his buckets (a wholesaler = seller + investor buckets, skip buyer). Frame "ROI" in the reader's units: cost per lead, cost per deal, not ROAS.
- **Service reference**: none
- **Claims to use**: Quality Score → lower CPC relationship (Google Ads Help, sourced in dossier).
- **Snippet capture**: no
- **Watch out for**: "ROI" is fine as reader shorthand but pair it with cost-per-lead language; avoid "ROAS."
