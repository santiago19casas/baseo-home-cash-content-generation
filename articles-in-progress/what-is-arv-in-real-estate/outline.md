#### H3: Step 1: Pull the Right Comps
- **Word target**: ~120
- **Sub-bullets**: 3–5 SOLD comps (not active listings), last 3–6 months, within ~1 mile, same bed/bath and size band, and **in renovated condition** — the #1 rookie error is comping a future-renovated house against as-is sales. Where operators pull comps, category level only: MLS access through an agent, county public records, listing portals. NO tool walkthroughs.

#### H3: Step 2: Adjust for the Differences
- **Word target**: ~100
- **Sub-bullets**: adjust comps for sqft, lot size, garage, condition delta; price-per-sqft × subject footage as the cross-check that catches a bad comp set. Worked example: renovated comps at ~$200/sqft × 1,500 sqft ≈ $300K.

#### H3: Step 3: Settle on a Defensible Number
- **Word target**: ~110

### H2: Common ARV Mistakes That Kill Deals

- **Word target**: 160–200
- **Sub-bullets**: one-sentence setup + BULLETED LIST (5–6 items, 1–2 sentences each):
  1. Comping against unrenovated sales (as-is comps produce an as-is number, not an ARV).
  2. Using active listings instead of solds (asking prices are opinions; sold prices are facts).
  3. Stale comps in a shifting market (6-month-old comps in a cooling market bake in a value that's gone).
  4. Over-improving for the street ($350K renovation standard on a $280K street returns $280K).
  5. Taking the seller's or a guru's ARV at face value (whoever hands you an ARV has an incentive attached to it).
  6. Ignoring holding-time risk (every extra month of holding eats the spread the 70% buffer was protecting).
