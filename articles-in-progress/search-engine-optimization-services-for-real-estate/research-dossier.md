- [Carrot — How AI Is Reshaping SEO for Real Estate Investors](https://carrot.com/blog/how-ai-is-reshaping-seo-for-real-estate-investors-2/) — supports: **one Carrot customer received 26 of 45 leads in a 7-day period from ChatGPT**; **30%+ of top Google rankings for motivated-seller terms are Carrot sites**; AI Overviews now appear on nearly half of searches; Google launched AI Mode to all US searchers in May 2025. The niche's most-trusted source, and it's the one the ICP already reads.
- [Seer Interactive — How Traffic from ChatGPT Converts (case study)](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/case-study-6-learnings-about-how-traffic-from-chatgpt-converts) — supports: **ChatGPT traffic converted at 15.9% vs 1.76% for Google organic**; ChatGPT users viewed 2.3 pages/session, nearly double organic. ⚠️ **Cite with the caveat**: this is a single B2B software client, 1,370 AI conversions against ~14M organic sessions. The directional finding (AI traffic arrives pre-qualified) is what we're claiming, not that a cash buyer will see 15.9%. **The draft must carry the caveat or the claim is inflated.**
- [Ahrefs / industry SEO pricing surveys, via GoodFirms + Clutch aggregation](https://www.goodfirms.co/resources/seo-pricing-plans) — supports: the market retainer range. **Most common tier $1,500–$5,000/month (≈48% of agencies); Ahrefs survey of 439 SEO professionals found an average retainer of $3,209/month; Clutch shows ~$3,199 average monthly.** This describes the *market*, not BASEO's price list, so it is publishable per the pricing policy.
- [Real Estate Bees — Average Wholesale Assignment Fee (survey)](https://realestatebees.com/statistics/average-wholesale-assignment-fee/) — supports: **national average assignment fee ~$13,000**; range $3,000–$25,000 by market; highest in NC/GA (~$22,000), lowest in AZ (~$5,000). Use for the "one extra deal per month" math.

### Tier 3 (practitioners)

- [Promodo — PPC for Real Estate Investors: Google Ads Guide](https://www.promodo.com/blog/the-ultimate-ppc-guide-for-real-estate-investors) — supports: investors targeting off-market deals pay roughly **$2.50–$5.00 CPC** on "we buy houses cash," "sell my house as-is," "distressed property buyers"; investor campaign conversion 7–10% producing a **CPL of $28–$65**.
- [Webrageous — Google Ads for Real Estate Investors and Wholesalers](https://webrageous.com/blog/google-ads-for-real-estate-investors.htm) — supports: CPC for "sell house fast" **ranges roughly $12–$63** depending on market. Use this as the *contested-term* number and the Promodo range as the *broader investor keyword* number. The honest framing is a spread, not a single figure.
- [Visionary Marketing — SEO vs PPC: Cost, ROI & Performance Data (2026)](https://visionary-marketing.co.uk/blog/seo-vs-ppc-data-comparison-2026) — supports: **in real estate, organic leads cost 83% less than PPC leads** (the largest gap of any industry studied); **Carrot's Investor Performance Study: organic leads close 2× more profitably than PPC leads**; content-marketing CPL starts ~$80–$100 and falls to **$7–$30** once content gains authority; real estate SEO ROI by year three ≈ **1,389%**.

## BASEO capability hooks (Active brief)

- **Service(s) in play**: Diagnostic Audit · Technical SEO + Foundation · Programmatic City Pages · Seller-Situation Content · AI Search Optimization (AEO) · Editorial Backlinks + Local Authority · Lead-Focused Reporting · PPC Management.

- **WHAT + WHY hooks** (safe level, from `baseo-services-reference`):
  - **Diagnostic Audit**: send a domain, get a written audit in ~2 business days: the 3 biggest issues, the competitor keyword gap, the deal-math projection for that market, and the pricing for that situation. Free, no call, yours to keep. **Honest-broker line is safe and encouraged**: if the market doesn't support the math, the audit says so, and BASEO has turned clients down.
  - **Technical SEO + Foundation** (months 1–2): call tracking, form tracking, GA4, Search Console, schema on service pages, template content rewritten, GBP rebuilt. WHY: leads become countable and the site becomes readable before content scales. End state: a dashboard that shows leads, not impressions.
  - **Programmatic City Pages** (months 2–6, ongoing): one original page per market the investor actually buys in. WHY: 60%+ of organic traffic on successful investor sites lands on city pages, not the homepage; and swap-the-city-name templates are what Google's Scaled Content Abuse policy targets.
  - **Seller-Situation Content** (months 2–6): the canonical five — probate, foreclosure/pre-foreclosure, divorce, inherited property, problem tenants (extended: fire damage, code violations, tired landlord). WHY: the searcher's situation is urgent and specific, so these convert above average, and they feed internal links to city pages.
  - **AEO** (from month 3, on accounts by default): Q&A structure, citation-friendly schema, weekly AI-citation tracking per market across ChatGPT/Perplexity/Claude/AI Overviews, and AI referral attribution recovering visits GA4 mislabels as "Direct." WHY: the seller's question now resolves to a short cited list. **Limitation that must appear**: no one controls whether an AI cites a page. We optimize inputs and track outputs. Never promise citation.
  - **Editorial Backlinks + Local Authority** (from month 3): chambers of commerce, REIAs, local news, relevant local directories. Explicitly no link farms, no PBNs, no paid schemes.
  - **Lead-Focused Reporting** (monthly): leads with phone numbers, organic cost-per-lead trend, pages published, AI citations, next month's plan in plain English. Plus lead-intent classification so the highest-intent form fills get called first.
  - **PPC Management** (optional complement): motivated-seller campaigns run under the same leads/CPL/cost-per-deal lens as organic. Public expectation: most clients cut PPC spend 30–50% by months 6–9 as organic takes over. Never "kill PPC on day one."
  - **Cross-service facts** (safe anywhere): month-to-month, 30 days' written notice; written monthly Scope of Work with a refund if a deliverable is missed; market exclusivity per core metro; client owns all content, links, data, and reports.
  - **Public timeline arc** (safe): audit week 1 → foundation months 1–2 → city pages + situation pillars months 2–6 → AI layer months 3–9 → compounding month 9+. **Expectation language to mirror**: first leads typically month 3–5; real lead volume month 6–9; "anyone telling you leads in 30 days is talking about PPC or lying."
