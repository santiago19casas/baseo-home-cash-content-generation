## What "getting clients" means when you buy houses for cash

An agent chases listing clients. You chase motivated sellers: probate, foreclosure, divorce, inherited houses, tired landlords ready to be done. Same question every operator asks, where do the deals come from, but a different answer.

The job comes down to two things. Be findable when a seller starts looking, and follow up fast when one raises a hand.

Speed is not a soft skill here. Responding within five minutes can lift your contact rate by up to 900%, and 78% of sellers go with the first person who calls them back ([MarketWiz](https://marketwiz.ai/real-estate-lead-response-time-why-5-minutes-10x-more-conversions/)). A lead you answer next morning is often a deal a competitor already has under contract.

So judge every channel below on two numbers only: what it costs per lead, and what it costs per deal. Not clicks. Not impressions. Deals.

## How do real estate investors generate leads?

Investors generate motivated-seller leads through three buckets. Free and sweat-equity channels (driving for dollars, networking, organic search) cost time instead of money. Paid channels (Google Ads, paid social, bought lead lists) cost money and buy speed. Referral and repeat business costs almost nothing but takes a track record to build. The cheapest per deal over time is organic search. The fastest is paid.

Here is how the main channels compare.

|---|---|---|---|

## Free ways to get motivated seller leads

Free does not mean easy. These channels cost consistency instead of cash, which makes them the right start when your budget is small. Two very different things live under "free," though: grinding for leads by hand, and being found online while you sleep.

### Driving for dollars and local networking

Drive your target neighborhoods and log every distressed or absentee house: overgrown lawns, boarded windows, code-violation signs. Skip trace the owners, then reach out. Work the rooms where sellers and referral sources already are: REIA meetings, foreclosure auctions, contractor and wholesaler networks.

This channel gives you total control and near-zero cost. It also never stops needing your time, and the volume stays low. Capture every lead in one place and follow up within 24 hours, or the effort is wasted. This is a tactic you run yourself. It is not something BASEO delivers.

### Get found on Google with local SEO and city pages

When a seller decides to sell, they type "sell my house fast [city]" or "we buy houses [city]" into Google. You want to be the result they find. That means a unique page for every market you buy in, seller-situation pages for the searches sellers type under stress (probate, foreclosure, divorce, inherited, problem tenants), and a real Google Business Profile.

The common mistake is a template. Swap-the-city-name Carrot pages read as duplicates, and Google's Scaled Content Abuse policy, live since May 2024, specifically targets large batches of unoriginal pages ([Google Search Central](https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2024/03/core-update-spam-policies)). When 30 competitors run the same template, Google can't tell you apart, so it ranks none of you.

Original-per-market is why real pages win. One Florida cash buyer went from 3 motivated seller leads a month to 28 in nine months, same market, no extra ad spend, at $161 per organic lead and falling (BASEO client data). That is the compounding channel doing its job.

Sellers no longer only search Google. They ask ChatGPT and read Google's AI Overview: "how do I sell my house fast for cash." The AI answers with a short list of companies. Being on that list is a new channel, and right now it is nearly free because most investors have no AI presence at all.

## Paid ways to get motivated seller leads

Money buys speed and volume. It does not always buy quality, and it never buys you an asset. Only turn paid on once your tracking and your follow-up are already working, because paid magnifies whatever system you have, good or broken.

### Google Ads for "sell my house fast"

This is the highest-intent paid channel and one of the priciest. Clicks on "sell my house fast" run $30 to $80 and up, and a managed campaign lands leads at roughly $80 to $300 each ([Lead Farmers PPC](https://www.leadfarmersppc.com/post/how-much-should-real-estate-investors-spend-on-google-ads)). Those are motivated sellers actively looking, which is why the price is high.

To make it pay, you need tight keyword targeting, a dedicated landing page, and five-minute follow-up. Skip any of the three and you fund junk clicks.

Here is the honest frame. Paid keeps the phone ringing today, but it does not get cheaper on its own, and the leads stop the day you stop paying. BASEO runs motivated-seller ads under the same cost-per-lead and cost-per-deal lens as organic, and plans for paid spend to shrink as the organic channel compounds. We would rather run your ads efficiently while the durable asset gets built than pretend paid is the finish line.

### Buying motivated-seller lead lists

Pay-per-lead services and shared-lead portals sell you a seller who filled out a form. The model is simple, but the catch is real: many of those leads are sold to several buyers at once and skew low-intent, so they demand heavy, fast follow-up to convert at all.

The verdict depends on your setup. If you run a high-volume operation with a dialer and a follow-up system, bought lists can feed it. If you don't have that system yet, you will pay for leads and watch them go cold. One note for clarity: BASEO does not sell leads. This is a channel you buy elsewhere, not a service we provide.

## How much do motivated seller leads cost?

The number that decides your marketing is cost per deal, not cost per lead. A $150 lead sounds fine until you learn it takes 20 of them to close one deal.

|---|---|---|---|
| Google Ads | $80–$300 | Thousands | Stops when you stop paying |
| Bought shared lists | $30–$100+ | High, low intent | Heavy follow-up required |
| Mature local SEO | Toward $161, falling | Hundreds to low thousands | Falls as pages compound |
| Referrals | ~$0 | Lowest | Requires a track record |
*Cost per deal is the metric that matters. Paid channels hold their price; organic gets cheaper as the pages age.*

That last row is the whole argument. Paid cost per lead holds steady or rises. Organic cost per lead falls every month the pages keep working. The Florida client hit $161 per organic lead by month 9 and it was still dropping (BASEO client data). At a typical assignment fee, one closed deal covers many months of the work that produced it.

## How long until your first deal?

Honest ranges, by channel. Driving for dollars and networking can surface a lead in weeks. Paid ads can produce one in days. Organic search takes 3 to 6 months to bring first leads and 6 to 9 to reach real volume. AI citation lands in a similar 6 to 9 month window.

Anyone promising motivated seller leads in 30 days is describing paid ads or selling you a story. Real organic volume compounds, which is exactly why it takes a couple of quarters to show and then keeps paying.

The Florida client's timeline is a fair benchmark: first organic leads around month 5, twelve of them plus the first three deals, then 28 leads a month by month 9 (BASEO client data). Slow, then sudden.

## Common lead-generation mistakes cash buyers make

- **Chasing every channel at once.** Running six half-built channels loses to one channel built to maturity. Pick the one that fits your budget and go deep.
- **Spending on ads before tracking is installed.** Without call and form tracking, you are guessing at cost per deal. Guessing is how budget disappears.
- **Quitting organic before month 6.** The channel compounds right after most people give up on it. You are paying for months 1 through 5 to get months 6 through 24.
- **Buying shared leads with no follow-up system.** 80% of sales need five or more follow-ups ([AgentZap](https://agentzap.ai/blog/real-estate-lead-statistics)). Buy leads without a system and you fund a competitor's pipeline.
- **Running a template site.** A swap-the-city-name Carrot page looks like 30% of your competitors, and Google ranks none of you. Original pages per market are the fix.
- **Ignoring the AI-search window.** Every month you wait, a competitor becomes the answer ChatGPT gives. First-mover advantage in this channel is real and temporary.

## Frequently asked questions

### How do real estate investors generate leads?

Through three buckets: free and sweat-equity channels (driving for dollars, networking, organic search), paid channels (Google Ads, paid social, bought lists), and referrals from past sellers. The cheapest per deal over time is organic search. The fastest is paid ads.

### What's the cheapest way to get motivated seller leads?

Short term, driving for dollars and local networking cost time instead of money, so they are cheapest on a small budget. Long term, a compounding organic website is cheapest per deal, because its cost per lead falls the longer it runs.

### How much do motivated seller leads cost?

Paid Google Ads leads run roughly $80 to $300 each, and bought shared lists run $30 to $100 and up. Organic leads cost more up front but fall over time. One BASEO client reached $161 per organic lead by month 9, still dropping.

### How long does SEO take to bring in seller leads?

Expect first leads in 3 to 6 months and real volume by months 6 to 9. Organic compounds rather than switches on, so it starts slow and then keeps paying. Anyone promising seller leads in 30 days means paid ads.

### Should I do PPC or SEO first?

Run both if you can. Use PPC for speed now, while SEO builds the asset that lowers your cost per deal over time. As organic compounds, shrink your paid spend. Paid rents the phone ringing; organic owns it.

## The bottom line

The channels that feel fastest, paid ads and bought lists, are the ones you rent, and the rent never drops. The channel that lowers your cost per deal is the one you own: an organic and AI-search presence that gets cheaper every month it runs.
