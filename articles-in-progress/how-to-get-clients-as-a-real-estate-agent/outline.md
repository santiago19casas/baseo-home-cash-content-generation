### H2: Rented leads vs. an asset that compounds

- **Word target**: 210–250 (BASEO-protagonist section + expertise close candidate)
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. The frame that decides where to put your money: PPC and bought lists are rent. The leads stop the day the payment stops, and the price never drops on its own.
  2. Organic search and AI presence are equity. Every page you publish keeps working, and cost per lead falls as the site ages.

### H2: Common lead-generation mistakes cash buyers make

- **Word target**: 190–220
- **Sub-bullets** (bulleted list, 6 items, 1–2 sentences each):
  1. Chasing every channel at once instead of building one to maturity.
  2. Spending on ads before call/form tracking is installed, so no one knows the cost per deal.
  3. Quitting organic before month 6, right before it compounds.
  4. Buying shared leads with no follow-up system, then blaming the channel (80% of sales need 5+ follow-ups — [AgentZap](https://agentzap.ai/blog/real-estate-lead-statistics)).
  5. Running a template Carrot site that looks like 30% of competitors, so Google ranks none of you (Scaled Content Abuse).
  6. Ignoring the AI-search window while a competitor becomes the cited answer.
- **BASEO reference**: contextual (count 9)
