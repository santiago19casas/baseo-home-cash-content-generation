#### H3: REsimpli: Best All-in-One Real Estate Investing CRM
- 120–150w. List building, skip tracing included, native dialer, direct mail drips, D4D, accounting + KPI dashboard, AI agents (calls/appointments). Pricing $149–$599/mo, 30-day trial (cite resimpli.com/pricing). Pro: replaces 5–6 tools. Con: accounting/portfolio thin for heavy buy-and-hold. Verdict: default for wholesalers/cash buyers. Keyword: best real estate investing crm.

#### H3: InvestorFuse (Carrot CRM): Best for Follow-Up Discipline
- 100–130w. Thesis: deals die from bad follow-up, not lead shortage. Structured workflows, per-member accountability. Pricing ~$147–$377 (cite carrot.com/crm-pricing); now lives under Carrot CRM, ~20% off for Carrot subscribers (from ~$118). Con: no native skip tracing/dialer. Verdict: teams sitting on hundreds of unconverted leads. Internal link: "90-day follow-up cadence" → /resources/how-to-convert-leads-in-real-estate/

#### H3: FreedomSoft: Best for Established Data Workflows
- 90–110w. CRM-first, phone system, workflow automation, e-signature. From ~$147/mo annual, ~$197–$297 monthly (cite freedomsoft.com/pricing). Con: no native list builder/skip tracing; dated interface. Verdict: strong pipeline if data sourcing solved (PropStream/BatchLeads).

#### H3: REI BlackBook: Best Built-In Phone System
- 90–110w. Profit Dial (calls, tracking, mass texting), campaign automation, website included. Solo ~$149/mo, top tier $799 (cite reiblackbook.com/pricing — note: differs from brief's $97–$297, use verified). Con: pay-as-you-go calls/texts on lower tiers; advanced automation in higher tiers. Verdict: phone-heavy operators.

#### H3: DealMachine: Best for Driving for Dollars
- 90–110w. Mobile-first: pin distressed properties on route, instant skip tracing, automated mail. ~$99–$232/mo annualized ($119–$279 monthly; cite dealmachine.com/pricing). Con: thin as a complete CRM; winning stack = DealMachine capture + REsimpli/FreedomSoft management. Verdict: essential if D4D is the primary channel, insufficient alone.

#### H3: Left Main REI (Salesforce): Best for Scaling Teams
- 80–100w. REI overlay on Salesforce: deep customization, enterprise reporting, unlimited scale. Con: high cost (expect $300–$1,000+/mo with implementation, hedged estimate), needs an admin. Verdict: 10+ person teams at institutional volume.

#### H3: Podio: Best Low-Cost Custom Build
- 90–110w. The REI community classic: no-code blank canvas; with add-ons (REI Automation Squad type) a full CRM at roughly $25–$50/mo (hedged). Con: build everything from scratch, no native skip tracing/mail, constant maintenance; teams migrating to AI-first platforms. Verdict: technical profile + time only.

#### H3: GoHighLevel: Best for Tech-Savvy Operators
- 80–100w. Max platform flexibility (funnels, SMS, email, pipelines), $97–$297/mo (cite HighLevel pricing guide). Con: weeks of setup + external data tools ($300–$500/mo extra, hedged). Verdict: technical operators/agencies, not start-tomorrow buyers.

#### H3: Forefront CRM: Best Visual Pipeline
- 70–90w. Drag-and-drop visual pipeline, follow-up automation, short learning curve. No pricing numbers (not verified). Verdict: solos and small teams who want the business at a glance.

#### H3: HubSpot / Pipedrive: Best Generic (Free) Starting Points
- 90–110w. PAA free-CRM answer: HubSpot free tier; Pipedrive from ~$14/user/mo annual (cite G2); Zoho free up to 3 users (mention). Lacks REI features (skip tracing, list stacking, direct mail) so you eventually migrate. Verdict: better than Excel, worse than any investor CRM at volume.
