- Answer (~60 words): There's no single best; it depends on your model. The best company for you reports leads (not impressions), works month-to-month, owns your niche, and proves results in writing. - Answer (~55 words): Real estate SEO typically runs $800–$10,000/month depending on market and scope; specialist investor retainers commonly sit at $2,000–$6,000. Pricing should be custom to your market and your competition, not a flat menu. One closed deal usually covers several months of the investment.

#### H3: How long until real estate SEO generates leads?
- Answer (~55 words): Expect the first organic leads around months 3–5 and meaningful lead volume by months 6–9, with foundation work in months 1–2. Competitive markets take longer. Any company promising leads in 30 days is describing paid ads, not SEO.

#### H3: Do SEO companies work for real estate investors?
- Answer (~55 words): Yes, when they specialize. Investor SEO is its own discipline: city pages, seller-situation pages, and AI-search optimization aimed at "sell my house fast" intent. One BASEO client went from 3 to 28 motivated seller leads a month in nine months. Generalist agent-focused agencies rarely deliver this.

#### H3: What is GEO/AEO and does my real estate site need it?
- Answer (~60 words): GEO/AEO (generative/answer engine optimization) is getting your pages cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews when someone asks how to sell or buy. It matters because AI traffic converts far higher than search: ChatGPT visitors converted at 15.9% vs 1.76% for Google organic in one study. If sellers use AI, yes.
