- **Word target**: 120–150

### H2: How to Capture and Score Leads

- **Word target**: 120–150
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Lead capture = the form/portal/call that puts a new lead in the CRM automatically. Lead scoring = the CRM ranks leads by behavior so you know who's hot.
  2. Behavioral signals: site visits, email opens, saved/viewed properties. Concrete example: a lead who views 5 listings in one ZIP in a night is telling you they're ready — call that one first.
  3. Rule: work the hot leads first; a scored list beats a first-in-first-out list every time.
  4. Contextual parallel (NOT a product claim): scoring by intent is the same logic BASEO uses when it classifies inbound form-fills against motivated-seller signals in its reporting, so the highest-intent seller gets called first. (This is 1 light BASEO thread — keep to a single clause.)
- **Internal links**: none required
- **External links**: none required
- **BASEO reference**: contextual parallel only (count 1/3, single clause)
- **Snippet capture**: no

### H2: How to Nurture Leads with Automated Follow-Ups

- **Word target**: 150–180
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Define real estate lead nurturing: regular, valuable contact from first inquiry through the close and past it. Most sellers aren't ready the day they fill out a form; nurturing is how you're the one they call when they are.
  2. Deal-math: 80% of sales close after the 5th contact, yet 48% of people never follow up even once — source ProfitOutreach. The deal is usually in the follow-up nobody sends.
  3. Model sequence, 3–5 messages over 1–2 weeks: (1) short personal intro + one question about their goal/timeline; (2) a curated market update or relevant opportunity for their area/situation; (3) a low-pressure CTA to a quick call.
  4. Rule: every message gives something (a number, an answer, an option). Don't just ask for the appointment five times.
- **Internal links**: optional "get motivated seller leads" if not already used twice.
- **External links**: ProfitOutreach 80%/48%.
- **BASEO reference**: none

- **Snippet capture**: no

### H2: How to Manage Your Pipeline Day to Day

- **Word target**: 110–140
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Daily routine: open the CRM, work today's task list, move deals to the right stage, log every conversation in a sentence or two.
  2. Fast logging matters: capturing a call should take seconds, not a 12-field form. If logging is a chore, you won't do it, and an un-logged pipeline is a guess.
  3. The visual pipeline tells you at a glance who's stuck where (three deals sitting in "Appointment" for two weeks is a signal).
  4. Mobile app: log the call from the truck or the driveway right after a drive-for-dollars stop, while it's fresh.
