- **BASEO frame**: contextual mention only, NOT the method. BASEO is named 1–3 times total, and only in the soft-CTA section and (at most) one earlier contextual aside. The article never claims BASEO builds, sets up, or runs a CRM.
- **BASEO connection (mandatory)**: A CRM full of leads is useless if no leads are coming in; BASEO's SEO/AEO fills the top of that pipeline with organic and AI-search motivated-seller leads, which is the input the whole CRM workflow depends on.
- **Adjacent active service(s) this primes**: AI Search Optimization (AEO), Programmatic City Pages, Lead-Focused Reporting (lead-intent classification parallels the lead-scoring section).

## Angle / POV

This is not a "top 10 CRMs" listicle. It's an operator walking another operator through the actual workflow, lead in the door to closed deal to the past-client pipeline, with the deal-math reason each step matters (speed to lead, follow-up persistence, cost per deal). The differentiating beat: most guides stop at "buy a CRM." This one is honest that a CRM only multiplies the leads you already have. If the top of the funnel is empty, the slickest automation in the world has nothing to automate. That's where the one BASEO mention earns its place, in the soft CTA, not bolted onto every section.

- **Sub-guideline**: 90–120 words. Import all contacts with full data (contact prefs, property type, price range, timeline, original source). Dedupe and verify phones/emails BEFORE launching campaigns. Segment (buyers, sellers, past clients, sphere). Dirty data ruins every automation downstream.
- **Watch out for**: keep it concrete; segmentation examples in investor terms (motivated sellers, cash buyers list, past clients, sphere).

- **Sub-guideline**: 90–120 words. Connect CRM to website/IDX, social, paid ads, portals (Zillow, Realtor.com), open houses, referrals so leads flow in automatically. Mention email, calendar, MLS, transaction management integration. Manual entry kills efficiency and adds errors.
