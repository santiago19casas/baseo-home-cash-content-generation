### H2: Frequently Asked Questions
- **Word target**: ~40–55 words per answer, 7 questions, + FAQ schema (JSON-LD) note for the publisher.
- **H3 questions (reframed to investor)**:
  1. **How do real estate investors get their first deal?** — Most land it through their sphere of influence: people who already trust them. Announce that you buy houses for cash, ask to be kept in mind, and pair it with driving for dollars and fast follow-up. Warm referrals are the fastest path.
  2. **How do real estate investors generate leads?** — Through three buckets: free sweat-equity methods (sphere, driving for dollars, FSBOs, social, local SEO), paid sources (lead lists, portals, ads), and referrals from past sellers.   3. **How long does it take to get motivated seller leads?** — It varies by channel: sphere outreach, driving for dollars, and paid ads can produce leads in days to weeks; content 3–12 months; SEO 6–12 months; farming 12–18 months. Consistency beats the channel — most who quit do so too early.

  5. **How much do investors spend on leads?** — Widely. Many mid-career operators spend $1,000+/month on lists and ads; experienced buyers spend less as referrals carry the business. New operators can start at $0 with sphere, driving for dollars, and content.
  6. **Can a new investor get deals with no track record?** — Yes. Prove value upfront: make fair as-is cash offers, close on the seller's timeline, and answer questions honestly. Sellers care more about certainty and speed than your résumé. Lean on your network and show up online.

They shine for targeting specific neighborhoods, FSBOs, and expired listings. Lead with value, respect Do-Not-Call and local rules, and treat them as one channel, not the whole plan.
