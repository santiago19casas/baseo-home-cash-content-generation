- **Word target**: 100–130

#### H3: Pre-foreclosure & foreclosure leads
- **Word target**: 60–80
- Highest urgency; timeline pressure (owner racing the auction date); records from county notices of default; timing beats volume (a perfect list a week too late is worthless).
- **Internal links**: "find foreclosure listings" → where-to-find-foreclosure-listings; "pre-foreclosure timeline" → what-pre-foreclosure-means-for-buyers.

#### H3: Probate & inherited property leads
- **Word target**: 60–80
- Heirs who didn't ask for the house; often out-of-state, often high equity (owned free and clear for decades); source = probate court filings; outreach must be sensitive (someone died) — respectful framing.

#### H3: Absentee owner & tired landlord leads
- **Word target**: 50–70
- Non-owner-occupied; landlord burnout after a bad tenant or rising costs; pull from title company / county assessor records (owner mailing address ≠ property address).

#### H3: High-equity & free-and-clear leads
- **Word target**: 50–70
- Most negotiating room (no mortgage to pay off, so more flexible on price/terms); rarely motivated alone; strongest when paired with another trigger.

#### H3: Tax-delinquent, divorce & code-violation leads
- **Word target**: 60–80
- Group the situational distress signals; each maps to a public record source (county tax rolls, court dockets, municipal code enforcement). Each is a reason-to-sell, not just a name.

- **Word target**: 180–220

### H2: Buying leads vs. generating your own: the real cost

- **Word target**: 160–200 (PIVOTAL)
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Buying = instant flow, but the lead is shared/rented; price rises as more investors bid the same list; the moment you stop paying, leads stop.
  2. Every lead you buy is a lead a competitor can also buy. You're renting attention.
  3. Generating your own = slower to start, but the channel is owned and compounds; a ranked page is inbound that can't be resold to your competitor.
  4. Template shortcut fails: 30%+ of top "we buy houses" sites run near-identical Carrot template content, so Google ranks none of them well (Carrot).
  5. The smart play is **bi-velocity**: PPC/LSA/GBP for fast leads *now* while SEO/AEO compounds into the owned channel. Not either/or.
  6. NO ROI promise. Frame honestly: paid buys speed, owned buys durability.
- **Data**: organic leads 83% cheaper than PPC, close 2× more profitably, mature content CPL $7–$30 ([Visionary Marketing](https://visionary-marketing.co.uk/blog/seo-vs-ppc-data-comparison-2026)); 30%+ template duplication ([Carrot](https://carrot.com/blog/how-ai-is-reshaping-seo-for-real-estate-investors-2/)). Optional first-party: Florida 3→28 in 9 months, no extra ad spend (BASEO client data) — use once.

### H2: How much do real estate leads cost?

- **Word target**: 140–170
- **Sub-bullets**:
  1. Public range for motivated seller leads: ~$25–$40 national/shared → ~$80–$120 county-exclusive → $300+ premium exclusive.
  2. Agent-focused leads for contrast: paid social $5–$30, Google buyer $20–$60, Zillow/realtor.com $100–$300.
  3. The number that matters is **cost per closed deal**, not sticker CPL. A $300 exclusive lead that closes beats twenty $25 shared leads that don't.
  4. Cheaper leads = more competition + more contacts to close; exclusive = fewer contacts, higher sticker.
- **Data**: [UndervaluedX](https://blog.undervaluedx.com/lead-generation/cost-per-lead) (tiers), [US Lead List](https://usleadlist.com/resources/exclusive-motivated-seller-leads) ($300–$500 exclusive), [HouseCashin](https://housecashin.com/motivated-seller-leads/) (from $66), [Ylopo](https://www.ylopo.com/blog/how-much-do-real-estate-leads-cost) / [The Close](https://theclose.com/zillow-premier-agent-worth-cost/) (agent pricing), [Promodo](https://www.promodo.com/blog/the-ultimate-ppc-guide-for-real-estate-investors) (PPC CPL).
